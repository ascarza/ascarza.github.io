About Me

Professor of Business Administration, Harvard Business School

I am a marketing professor at Harvard Business School where I research, teach, and think about how companies can better manage customers for growth, and how data and artificial intelligence (AI) can enable them to do so.

My research interests include customer retention, personalization and targeting, AI in Marketing, and algorithmic bias. I enjoy helping companies make sense of their data to better understand their customers, measure and assess their value, and quantify the impact of their actions and interventions.

I am a co-founder of the Customer Intelligence Lab at the D³ Institute at HBS where we conduct research with the goal of helping organizations use their valuable customer data effectively and responsibly.

Research

Working Papers

Policy-Aware Experimentation: Strategic Sampling for Optimized Targeting Policies [Show Abstract]
Yi-Wen Chen, Eva Ascarza and Oded Netzer (2025) [Paper]
This paper develops a framework for designing experiments that optimize both learning and targeting outcomes. We show how strategic sampling decisions during experimentation can significantly improve the effectiveness of subsequent targeting policies.
Improving Targeting with Privacy-Protected Data: Honest Calibration of Treatment Effects [Show Abstract]
Ta-Wei Huang and Eva Ascarza (2025) [Paper]
This research addresses the challenge of personalized targeting when customer data is privacy-protected. We develop methods for honest calibration of treatment effects that maintain targeting effectiveness while respecting privacy constraints.
Dynamic Personalization with Multiple Customer Signals: Multi-Response State Representation in Reinforcement Learning [Show Abstract]
Liangzong Ma, Ta-Wei Huang, Eva Ascarza and Ayelet Israeli (2025) [Paper]
We propose a reinforcement learning approach for dynamic personalization that effectively integrates multiple customer signals. Our framework uses multi-response state representation to improve personalization decisions over time.
Incrementality Prediction: Synergizing Past Experiments for Intervention Personalization [Show Abstract]
Ta-Wei Huang, Eva Ascarza and Ayelet Israeli (2025) [Paper]
This paper develops a method to predict treatment incrementality by leveraging information from past experiments. We show how synthesizing insights across multiple experiments improves intervention personalization and resource allocation.
Learning When to Quit in Sales Conversations [Show Abstract]
Emaad Manzoor, Eva Ascarza and Oded Netzer (2025) [Paper] [Website]
We develop AI agents that learn optimal stopping decisions in sales conversations. Our approach identifies when to continue or terminate conversations to maximize efficiency and customer experience.
In Privacy We Trust: The Effect of Privacy Regulations on Data Sharing Behavior [Show Abstract]
Ozge Demirci, Eva Ascarza and Ayelet Israeli (2025) [Paper]
This research examines how privacy regulations affect consumer willingness to share data. We analyze the impact of regulatory frameworks on trust and data sharing behavior across different contexts.
Protected Heterogeneity: A Variance-Based Framework for Fair Algorithmic Personalization [Show Abstract]
Noah Ahmadi, Eva Ascarza and Ayelet Israeli (2025) [Paper]
We propose a variance-based framework to ensure fairness in algorithmic personalization. Our approach balances personalization effectiveness with fairness constraints to prevent discrimination while maintaining performance.
Learning from Many Experiments: A Hierarchical Bayesian Framework for Decomposing Marketing Treatment Heterogeneity [Show Abstract]
Peter Ebbes, Eva Ascarza and Oded Netzer (2025)
This paper develops a hierarchical Bayesian framework to decompose treatment heterogeneity across multiple marketing experiments. We show how to synthesize learnings from many experiments to better understand heterogeneous treatment effects.
